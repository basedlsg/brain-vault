# A vault stops being an archive the moment it has feedback

Most people's "second brain" is a write-only system. Information goes in, nothing comes out, the vault becomes a bookmarking tool.

The unlock isn't more capture. It's the **return path**. Daily briefs that surface yesterday's connections to today's problems. Weekly syntheses that find the thesis I'm building toward without having stated it.

What I want to test over the next 90 days: does the compounding effect actually show up at month 3 the way it's claimed? My hypothesis is that without active prompting (the daily brief, not just "search when I remember to"), even a well-organized vault decays into a graveyard within ~6 weeks.

The whole architecture here is a bet on that hypothesis: capture is automated so it doesn't compete with attention; the brief runs without me asking; CLAUDE.md keeps Claude's context fresh so its connections aren't stale.

If by 2026-08-09 (90 days) I have not had a single "wait, the vault just told me something I didn't know about my own thinking" moment — the system has failed and I should kill it.
